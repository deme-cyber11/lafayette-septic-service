Commercial Grease Trap Pumping in Lafayette

Every restaurant, commercial kitchen, school cafeteria, and food-service business in Lafayette Parish is required to manage fats, oils, and grease (FOG) discharged into the wastewater system. Grease traps and grease interceptors capture these substances before they enter the sewer or septic system, where they would otherwise solidify, cause blockages, and create sanitary sewer overflows. Regular cleaning is not just smart maintenance — it's a code requirement enforced by the Lafayette Utilities System and the Louisiana Department of Environmental Quality.

Bayou Teche Septic provides scheduled grease trap pumping for restaurants throughout the Lafayette area. We service traps of all sizes, from compact under-sink units in small cafes to large outdoor interceptors at major restaurants and institutional kitchens. Every pumping comes with proper manifesting and documentation so you have the records you need for health inspections and code compliance.

Why Lafayette Restaurants Need Regular Grease Trap Service

A neglected grease trap is one of the most common causes of kitchen plumbing emergencies. When grease builds up past the trap's design capacity, it begins flowing into the downstream drains, where it cools, hardens, and forms blockages that can shut down service, cause backups into the kitchen, and trigger fines from local authorities. Lafayette's enforcement of FOG ordinances has tightened in recent years, with restaurants required to maintain pumping records and demonstrate ongoing compliance during inspections.

Signs Your Grease Trap Needs Service

  • Slow drains in kitchen sinks, dishwashers, or floor drains
  • Foul odors near the trap or in the kitchen
  • Visible grease layer thicker than 25% of the trap's depth
  • It has been more than 90 days since your last cleaning
  • An upcoming health inspection or code review
  • New ownership or change of operator requiring documented compliance

Our Grease Trap Cleaning Process

Our crews arrive with a dedicated vacuum truck and the equipment to pump traps of any size. We remove all grease, food solids, and wastewater from the trap, scrape down the walls and baffles, inspect for damage, and refill the trap with clean water so it's ready to perform from the moment we leave. You receive a written service ticket and waste manifest for your records.
