Comprehensive Septic System Inspections

A professional septic inspection gives you a clear picture of your system's health and helps you avoid expensive surprises down the road. Whether you are buying or selling a home, applying for a permit, or simply want to know the condition of your septic system, Bayou Teche Septic provides thorough evaluations that cover every critical component from tank to drain field.

In Lafayette Parish, where high water tables and clay-rich soils put constant pressure on septic systems, routine inspections are especially important. Many systems in our area were installed decades ago and may not meet current Louisiana Department of Health standards. An inspection identifies problems before they escalate into full system failures that can cost thousands of dollars to repair or replace.

When You Need a Septic Inspection

  • Buying a home with a septic system in Lafayette or surrounding areas
  • Selling your property and need a system evaluation for disclosure
  • Routine maintenance check every 2 to 3 years as recommended by the LDH
  • Applying for a building permit or property transfer
  • Noticing signs of system trouble such as odors or slow drains
  • Planning an addition or remodel that increases water usage

What Our Inspection Covers

Our certified technicians perform a detailed evaluation of your entire septic system. We locate and uncover the tank, measure sludge and scum levels, check the condition of baffles and tank walls, inspect inlet and outlet pipes, and evaluate the drain field for signs of saturation or failure. We also check for proper grading and drainage around the system and review any visible components such as distribution boxes and risers.

For real estate transactions, our inspection reports are thorough enough to satisfy lenders, buyers, and agents. Each report includes a written summary of findings, photos of key components, measurements, and clear recommendations for any needed repairs or maintenance. We explain everything in plain language so you understand exactly what is happening with the system.

Lafayette's Unique Inspection Challenges

South Louisiana's environment creates conditions that accelerate septic system wear. Our inspectors are specifically trained to look for issues common to the Lafayette area, including tank flotation caused by high groundwater, root intrusion from local tree species, soil compaction in clay-heavy lots, and drain field saturation during our frequent heavy rainfalls. These are problems that inspectors unfamiliar with our region often miss entirely.

Inspection Reports You Can Trust

After completing your inspection, we provide a detailed written report within 24 hours. The report covers tank condition and capacity, sludge and scum levels, baffle integrity, pipe connections, drain field performance, and any code compliance issues. We also include our professional recommendation for the next pumping or service interval and flag any repairs that should be addressed. Our reports are accepted by real estate agents, lenders, and insurance companies throughout Lafayette Parish and Acadiana.

What a Professional Septic Inspection Involves

A professional septic inspection goes well beyond a visual check of the access lid. When Bayou Teche Septic performs a full inspection, our technician locates all components of the system — tank, distribution box, and drain field — inspects the tank's interior condition and solid levels, checks baffles and outlet filters for integrity, assesses the drain field for signs of hydraulic failure or soil saturation, and evaluates all accessible plumbing connections for leaks or damage.

In south Louisiana's high water table environment, drain field assessment is especially important. We look for signs that effluent is surfacing, pooling, or failing to absorb properly — conditions that can lead to environmental health code violations and potentially costly remediation if left unaddressed. We also check for any signs of structural damage to the tank itself, which can occur over time in the shifting soils common across Lafayette Parish.

After the inspection, we provide a written report documenting the system's condition, any issues found, and our recommendations for maintenance or repair. If the inspection is part of a real estate transaction, our documentation meets standard buyer-seller requirements in Louisiana.
