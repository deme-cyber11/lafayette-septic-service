Aerobic Septic System Repair Near You in Lafayette

Your aerobic treatment unit (ATU) is the engine of your wastewater system — when one component fails, the entire system falls out of compliance fast. Bayou Teche Septic specializes in aerobic system repair across Lafayette Parish and surrounding Acadiana communities. We service every major ATU brand including Norweco Singulair, Delta Whitewater, Hoot, Clearstream, Jet, and Aqua-Safe. Whether your alarm is sounding, your spray heads have quit, or your air pump has gone silent, we diagnose the issue and get your system back in compliance the same day in most cases.

Aerobic systems are required across much of South Louisiana because our high water table and clay soils make conventional anaerobic drain fields impractical. That added complexity means more components that can fail — and Louisiana DHH regulations require your system to stay operational and under a current service contract. A neglected aerobic system does not just fail; it puts you at risk for fines, permit violations, and untreated effluent reaching your yard or neighboring properties.

Aerobic Components We Repair

  • Air pumps and compressors (Hiblow, Gast, Thomas, FPZ) — diaphragm rebuilds and full replacement
  • Aerators and venturi diffusers clogged with biomat or scale
  • Spray heads, spray lines, and irrigation field pumps
  • Chlorinators and chlorine feed tubes — tablet or liquid systems
  • Float switches, effluent pumps, and pump tank controls
  • Audible and visual alarm panels, control boards, and timers
  • Pretreatment tank baffles, filters, and transfer pumps
  • Disinfection chambers and UV units where installed

Warning Signs Your Aerobic System Needs Repair

Aerobic systems signal trouble long before a full failure — if you know what to watch for. Call us the moment you notice any of these symptoms. Catching an issue early is the difference between a $200 part and a $6,000 spray-field rebuild.

  • Flashing red alarm light or continuous audible alarm on your control panel
  • Sewage odor near the aerobic tank, spray heads, or pretreatment lid
  • Air pump running louder than usual or silent when it should be humming
  • Spray heads that no longer pop up or cycle on schedule
  • Soggy ground or standing effluent under the spray field
  • Cloudy, dark, or foul-smelling effluent at the discharge point
  • Chlorinator empty more quickly than normal — possible overflow or bypass
  • Repeated tripped breakers on the aerator or pump circuits
