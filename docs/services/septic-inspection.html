Thorough septic system inspections for homebuyers, real estate transactions, and routine maintenance. Detailed reports you can trust from Lafayette's experienced septic professionals.

Comprehensive Septic System Inspections

Whether you are buying a home, selling a property, or simply want to know the health of your existing system, a professional septic inspection gives you the information you need to make smart decisions. In Lafayette Parish, where high water tables and clay soils put extra strain on septic systems, knowing the true condition of your system is not just helpful, it is essential.

Our inspection process goes well beyond a surface-level check. We examine every major component of your septic system, including the tank interior, inlet and outlet baffles, distribution box, and drain field condition. We measure sludge and scum levels inside the tank, check for structural cracks or deterioration, verify proper flow between components, and assess whether the drain field is absorbing effluent as it should. For systems with pumps, alarms, or aerobic treatment units, we test each mechanical component for proper function.

Lafayette's unique geography makes inspections particularly important. The water table throughout much of Lafayette Parish sits just a few feet below the surface, especially during wet seasons. This high groundwater creates conditions where failing drain fields can contaminate nearby water sources, and where improperly sealed tanks can allow groundwater intrusion that overwhelms the system. Our inspectors understand these local conditions and know exactly what to look for in systems operating in this environment.

After every inspection, you receive a detailed written report documenting the condition of each component, photographs of key findings, and clear recommendations for any maintenance or repairs needed. For real estate transactions, our reports meet the documentation requirements expected by lenders, buyers, and real estate attorneys throughout Louisiana. We provide straightforward, honest assessments so you can move forward with confidence.

What Our Inspection Covers

Every septic inspection is thorough, covering each critical component of your system from the house connection to the drain field.

1

Tank Condition

We open the tank and examine walls, floor, and lid for cracks, corrosion, root intrusion, or structural compromise that could lead to leaks or failure.

2

Baffles & Tees

Inlet and outlet baffles are inspected to confirm they are intact and functioning. Missing or damaged baffles allow solids to escape into the drain field.

3

Sludge & Scum Levels

We measure sludge depth at the bottom and scum thickness at the surface to determine if the tank needs pumping and how well it has been maintained.

4

Distribution Box

The distribution box is checked for level placement, structural integrity, and equal flow to all drain field lines, ensuring even effluent distribution.

5

Drain Field Assessment

We evaluate the drain field for signs of saturation, ponding, odors, or unusually green vegetation that indicates effluent surfacing above the soil.
