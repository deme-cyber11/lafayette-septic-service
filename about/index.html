Our Story

Bayou Teche Septic was built on a simple idea: homeowners in Lafayette Parish deserve a septic company they can actually trust. Too many families have been overcharged, given the runaround, or left waiting by companies that treat septic work as an afterthought. We set out to change that by providing professional, transparent septic services rooted in real local knowledge.

Working across Lafayette Parish, we understand the unique challenges that come with maintaining septic systems in South Louisiana. Our region's clay-heavy soils, high water table, and frequent heavy rainfall all put extra stress on drain fields and septic tanks. We know these conditions firsthand because we live and work here. That local expertise means we can spot problems other companies miss and recommend solutions that actually hold up in Louisiana's demanding environment.

We stay current with all Louisiana Department of Health (LDH) regulations and permitting requirements so our customers never have to worry about compliance. From routine pumping to full system installations, every job is handled with the same commitment to honest pricing, clear communication, and quality workmanship. Whether you are in the heart of Lafayette or out in the surrounding parishes, we treat every property like it is our own.

Deep Roots in South Louisiana's Septic Industry

Bayou Teche Septic has been serving Lafayette Parish and the surrounding Acadiana region for years, and in that time we've built a reputation that speaks for itself. Septic service is a trust business — homeowners don't call us because they're excited about it. They call when something's wrong, often at an inconvenient time, and they need a company that will show up promptly, diagnose the problem honestly, and fix it without padding the bill.

South Louisiana's climate and soil conditions create unique challenges for septic systems. The high water table across much of Lafayette Parish means systems must be designed and maintained with those conditions in mind. Seasonal flooding, saturated soils after heavy rain, and the high temperatures that accelerate bacterial activity all affect how septic systems perform. Our team understands these dynamics and applies that local knowledge to every service call.

We handle everything from routine pumping and inspection to full system repair and emergency drain field assessment. We're licensed, insured, and committed to transparent pricing — you'll know what the job costs before we start any work.
