Reliable Septic Service for New Iberia Homeowners

New Iberia is the parish seat of Iberia Parish and the largest city in the area outside of Lafayette, with around 28,000 residents. The city is known for its beautiful historic downtown along the Bayou Teche, the Shadows-on-the-Teche plantation home, and its proximity to Avery Island, home of Tabasco hot sauce. New Iberia itself has municipal sewer in much of the city, but the surrounding rural areas — including communities like Loreauville, Jeanerette, Lydia, and the unincorporated stretches along Highway 14 — are heavily dependent on individual septic systems.

Iberia Parish sits on alluvial soils deposited by Bayou Teche and the Atchafalaya river system over thousands of years. These soils are predominantly heavy clay with poor natural drainage, and the water table is often quite close to the surface, particularly in lower-lying areas near the bayou and toward the coast. Aging homes throughout the parish may have undersized concrete tanks, while sugar mill workers' housing and former plantation properties along the bayou often have systems that have not been professionally serviced in many years. Even newer construction must be designed carefully to handle the wet conditions common across the parish.

Bayou Teche Septic serves New Iberia homeowners and the broader Iberia Parish rural community with a complete range of septic services: routine pumping, professional inspections for home sales, repair of cracked tanks and failed components, drain field restoration when chronic flooding overwhelms the soil, new septic system installations for new construction or replacement of failed systems, and grease trap cleaning for the many restaurants in and around the city.

From the historic homes of Main Street to the working sugar mills and rural subdivisions throughout the parish, we deliver dependable septic service with honest pricing. We also serve Jeanerette, Loreauville, Lydia, Avery Island, and surrounding Iberia Parish communities, along with all of Lafayette Parish. Call (337) 492-0960 for septic pumping, inspection, or repair.
